## TL;DR

The new plane was built as **an intent classifier on top of old Kobe + old
Fraser handlers**, with the new Miya synthesizer voicing the response.
That design is correct for *open-ended coaching queries* (the "orchestrate"
path). It is **wrong for everything else**.

Most of the user's Telegram messages route to `kobe_route` or
`fraser_route`, which **bypass the synthesizer entirely** and pass the
agent's raw text straight to Telegram. That is the architectural source
of:

- The "fraser says: Venkat..." voice leak (Kobe's mesh-delegation prefix
  leaked verbatim because nothing scrubbed it).
- The wrong 1RMs (Kobe's reasoner hallucinated; new Miya never saw the
  output to fact-check).
- The generic warmup/cooldown (no user-profile injection on the kobe_route
  path).
- The active-rest = 0 kcal math bug (old Kobe bug; new plane is a
  passthrough so the bug ships).
- The pace contradiction ("646 ahead" vs ~520 behind) — Bug-H prevention
  fires only on the synth path; the kobe_route path has no arbitration.

Each of these is a symptom of the same structural choice: **passthrough
without re-voicing or fact-checking**.

## What's structurally wrong (and what would fix it for real)

### The fundamental issue

The new plane treats Kobe and Fraser as **black-box backends**. We send
them a prompt, they return a string, we ship that string. The synth-side
prompt rewrite, arbitration, fact-check, and voice rules **only apply
when we author the response ourselves** (the orchestrate path).

In the user's actual usage, most messages route to passthrough paths.
**Most of the prompt-engineering work we did tonight does not apply to
most replies.**

### What a robust architecture looks like

Three changes, in priority order:

1. **Single voice sink.** Every reply — including kobe_route and
   fraser_route — funnels through `synthesizer.synthesize()` or a
   post-processor that enforces voice + length + fact-check rules. The
   passthrough exists for latency; the voice contract must not.

2. **Canonical UserProfile injection.** A `vault/user_profile.json`
   (1RMs, mobility limitations, current weight, target, HRV/sleep trend,
   standing constraints like "newborn, sleep-deprived, no AM workouts")
   that gets pulled into the FACTS block of every prompt. The LLM stops
   hallucinating because the facts are there.

3. **Cross-validation layer.** Before sending to the user, the
   orchestrator runs a `validate(text, facts) -> (text, contradictions)`
   pass that catches pace contradictions, wrong 1RMs, and other
   discrepancies between the LLM's output and known facts. Arbitration
   becomes a property of every response, not just the synth path.

This is a **multi-week refactor**, not a tonight fix. The right sequence:

- Week 1: Build `vault/user_profile.json` + loader + prompt injection.
- Week 2: Move every reply through a single voice sink.
- Week 3: Add the cross-validation layer.
- Week 4: Eval against the 3 months of old-Miya transcripts as a
  regression bar.

### Why this didn't surface in pre-prod testing

Our test stack covers:
- Unit (function behavior)
- Contract (adapter shape)
- Eval (synth output quality — synth path only)
- Adversarial (prompt-injection — synth path only)
- Regression (specific bugs — most pinned on synth path)

We **do not** have:
- An end-to-end test that asks "if a real user sent this message, what
  text appears in Telegram?" — across all routing paths.
- A user-profile-grounding eval ("Did the bot use the right 1RMs?").
- A cross-path consistency eval ("Did /plan and 'where am i' agree?").

P0 for the next sprint: build the missing eval layer.

will not be tonight, this week, or next week. The Gemini coach you
showed me has months of accumulated context, real numbers, and a single

B. **Run new Miya v2 with eyes open** — tonight's scrubber kills the
